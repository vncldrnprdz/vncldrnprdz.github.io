Ventilation System with Self-Monitoring, Analysis, and Reporting Technology (S.M.A.R.T.) for Computer Laboratory

This thesis project, completed at Southern Luzon State University, focused on designing and developing a Ventilation System with Self-Monitoring, Analysis, and Reporting Technology (S.M.A.R.T.) specifically for computer laboratories. The research addressed the challenge of overheating and energy inefficiency in lab environments by creating a system that could automatically track and analyze laboratory conditions.

The system integrates an ESP32 microcontroller, LM35 temperature sensor, PIR motion sensor, and IR receiver to continuously monitor room temperature, detect occupancy, and verify air conditioning status. All gathered data is displayed on an LCD screen, automatically stored in Google Sheets, and made accessible through the Blynk mobile application. This combination ensures both real-time visibility and remote access to laboratory conditions, allowing users and administrators to make quick, informed decisions.

The device is very practical for computer laboratories requiring monitoring. Its real-time reporting ensures protection and comfort, I highly recommend it for institutions that value safety, efficiency, and innovation.

Yel Talabong

Computer Technology Student

The study highlights the potential of IoT-based solutions in academic facilities. The researchers recommend future enhancements, including fire detection features, automated air conditioner control, and integration with weather-based adaptive systems to further strengthen the impact and practicality of the S.M.A.R.T. device.

Shaped by discipline, values, and lifelong friendships.

In 2015, I entered the Sisters of Mary School – Adlas, Inc. in Silang, Cavite. It was a place unlike any other — a boarding school where thousands of young boys like me lived, studied, and grew together. Life there was strict but meaningful. We only had one visiting day each year to see our families, and just two to three weeks of vacation before returning to campus. Communication with the outside world was almost nonexistent — we were allowed to send just one letter per year.

Yet, despite being away from my family, those years turned out to be some of the best years of my life. Inside the walls of the campus, I learned the values that continue to guide me today — discipline, faith, perseverance, and gratitude. I met friends who became like brothers, bonds that I know will last a lifetime. We shared everything — the struggles, the laughter, and the dreams of building a better future.

The institution gave me opportunities I never thought I’d have. I was a constant academic awardee, even reaching Top 4 in my batch. I served as a class officer multiple times, took part in competitions, and received several awards and recognitions. Beyond academics, I got to experience things that both the rich and poor could relate to — the joy of trips outside campus, the excitement of learning new skills, and the sense of accomplishment in every achievement.

During my stay, I also became a student teacher, handling lessons in AutoCAD 2D and 3D whenever no teacher was available. This role gave me the chance to share knowledge while honing my own technical skills. I actively attended seminars, workshops, and trainings, joined student organizations, and even served as an officer in some of them — experiences that taught me responsibility, leadership, and service.

Looking back, the restrictions didn’t matter — no phones, no internet, no constant contact with the outside world. What mattered was what we gained inside: values, friendships, experiences, and a stronger sense of who we are. For me, The Sisters of Mary – Adlas, Inc. was not just a school. It was a home, a training ground for life, and the place that shaped me into the person I am today.
