Ventilation System with Self-Monitoring, Analysis, and Reporting Technology (S.M.A.R.T.) for Computer Laboratory

This thesis project, completed at Southern Luzon State University, focused on designing and developing a Ventilation System with Self-Monitoring, Analysis, and Reporting Technology (S.M.A.R.T.) specifically for computer laboratories. The research addressed the challenge of overheating and energy inefficiency in lab environments by creating a system that could automatically track and analyze laboratory conditions.

The system integrates an ESP32 microcontroller, LM35 temperature sensor, PIR motion sensor, and IR receiver to continuously monitor room temperature, detect occupancy, and verify air conditioning status. All gathered data is displayed on an LCD screen, automatically stored in Google Sheets, and made accessible through the Blynk mobile application. This combination ensures both real-time visibility and remote access to laboratory conditions, allowing users and administrators to make quick, informed decisions.

This device is very useful and practical, especially for computer laboratories that require constant monitoring. Its real-time reporting and accurate data help ensure both equipment protection and user comfort. I highly recommend it for institutions that value safety, efficiency, and innovation.

Yel Talabong

Computer Technology Student

The study highlights the potential of IoT-based solutions in academic facilities. The researchers recommend future enhancements, including fire detection features, automated air conditioner control, and integration with weather-based adaptive systems to further strengthen the impact and practicality of the S.M.A.R.T. device.

As a member of different rondalla ensembles, I was able to explore music not only as a personal passion but also as a collaborative art form. This experience not only enhanced my musical ability but also instilled resilience and determination to strive for excellence.

Ultimately, rondalla was not just an artistic activity, but a cultural and personal journey that strengthened both my creativity and sense of identity.
